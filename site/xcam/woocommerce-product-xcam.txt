PRODUCT TITLE
XCAM Prebuilt LAN ISR Camera Node

SHORT DESCRIPTION
XCAM is a prebuilt local-network camera node for XTOC Video ISR. It gives preparedness groups, labs, field teams, and event operators a small RTSP/MJPEG/snapshot camera endpoint with browser setup, location-friendly naming, and optional XTOC Sentinel motion alerts.

WHY TEAMS BUY XCAM
Most low-cost cameras either depend on vendor apps or become messy one-off devices with unclear addresses, unclear names, and unclear operating roles. XCAM is built for the opposite workflow: place the camera, name it for the site, connect it to the local network, and hand clear URLs to the TOC.

- Put eyes on gates, approaches, staging areas, equipment benches, vehicle lanes, storage rooms, temporary watch points, and training lanes.
- Use local RTSP, MJPEG, and snapshot URLs instead of depending on a cloud camera account.
- Give each camera a human-readable identity such as xcam-front-gate or xcam-yard-north so operators know what they are looking at.
- Feed XTOC Video ISR with camera sources that are simple to test and simple to replace.
- Enable optional camera-frame motion alerts that post XTOC T=11 SENTINEL packets to the local MANET bridge.

XCAM is the small camera edge that makes the video board easier to populate, label, and operate.

LOCAL-FIRST BY DESIGN
XCAM is not trying to be a consumer cloud camera. It is a practical LAN node:

- No cloud account required for local viewing.
- No subscription relay required for local operation.
- Simple URLs that can be copied into XTOC or tested in a browser.
- mDNS naming for friendly local discovery.
- IPv4 fallback for networks where mDNS is unreliable.

MOTION ALERTS FOR XTOC SENTINEL
When enabled, XCAM can compare sampled camera frames and post a structured XTOC Sentinel alert to the configured local MANET bridge URL.

- The camera frame itself is the motion input.
- Operators can tune sensitivity and cooldown for the site.
- Alerts use XTOC T=11 SENTINEL packet text so the event lands in the same operational ecosystem as other X Suite reports.
- This is useful for lightweight "something changed here" awareness, not as a certified alarm system.

SETUP PATH
<img class="aligncenter size-full" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/MKme/xcam/main/site/images/xcam-configuration.png" alt="XCAM browser configuration page" width="1365" height="768" />

1. Power the XCAM device.
2. Connect to the temporary xcam-&lt;suffix&gt; setup WiFi if it has not been provisioned yet.
3. Open the setup page and enter the local WiFi settings.
4. Set a meaningful thing name such as xcam-front-gate.
5. Reboot onto the permanent network.
6. Open the status page and verify the RTSP, MJPEG, and snapshot URLs.
7. Add the desired URL to XTOC Video ISR or any compatible local viewer.

OPERATIONAL NOTES
- XCAM is intended for trusted LAN, field LAN, lab, MANET, and controlled local-network deployments.
- Anyone who can reach the stream URL may be able to view the camera feed. Segment the network and place cameras accordingly.
- WiFi quality, power stability, antenna placement, and board variant affect real-world reliability.
- Motion alerts depend on camera view, lighting, sensitivity settings, and network reachability to the bridge.
- XCAM does not replace a hardened NVR, certified security camera system, or life-safety alarm system.

[SECTION: FAQ]
Q: Does XCAM require XTOC?
A: No. XCAM exposes normal local RTSP, MJPEG, and snapshot URLs. XTOC Video ISR is the preferred X Suite viewer, but compatible local viewers can use the same streams.

Q: Does XCAM require the internet?
A: No. XCAM is designed for local-network operation. Internet is not required for local viewing after the camera is configured.

Q: What makes XCAM different from a generic ESP32-CAM sketch?
A: XCAM is packaged around operational use: unique default identity, location-friendly naming, local status page URLs, RTSP/MJPEG/snapshot surfaces, broad camera tuning, and optional XTOC Sentinel motion-alert posting.

Q: Can several XCAM devices run on the same LAN?
A: Yes. Each device starts with a unique default identity and should be renamed to a physical location or role so operators can tell feeds apart.

Q: Can XCAM send motion alerts into XTOC?
A: Yes, when motion alerts are configured with a usable XTOC MANET bridge URL. The device sends XTOC T=11 SENTINEL packet text.

Q: Is this a cloud camera?
A: No. XCAM is meant to be a local LAN camera node. It does not need a cloud camera service for local viewing.

Q: Can I expose XCAM directly to the public internet?
A: That is not recommended. Use trusted LANs, VPNs, firewall rules, and network segmentation appropriate to your deployment.

Q: Does this include an XTOC license?
A: Not by default unless the store bundle explicitly says so. XCAM is the camera node; XTOC is the separate command-center software.

Q: What should I name each camera?
A: Use the physical role or location operators will recognize, such as xcam-front-gate, xcam-yard-north, xcam-drone-bench, or xcam-radio-room.

Q: Is motion detection a certified alarm system?
A: No. It is lightweight operational awareness based on camera-frame change detection and should not be treated as a certified life-safety or intrusion alarm system.
